# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Visitors who want a sense of who Niño is: peers, curious collaborators, and anyone landing from a link or search. They are not in a workflow or buying flow. They are browsing for personality, taste, and proof of craft. The job to be done is lightweight: understand what you build, how you think, and whether they want to stay connected (email, projects, studio work).

## Product Purpose

JuiceColored is Niño Mollaneda's personal presence on the web: a single home that carries voice, current work, and studio output without feeling like a sales deck. Success is someone leaving with a clear human impression (playful, honest, craft-forward) and optional paths to personal projects, alt164 studio apps, or contact. Conversion is secondary to presence; the site should feel lived-in, not optimized for a funnel.

## Brand Personality

Playful, honest, craft-forward. Warm and inviting without performative cheer. Indie-maker energy: personal, direct copy; real projects over positioning slides. The listening status and small human details should feel like someone is home, not a template filling hero slots.

## Anti-references

- Dark SaaS dashboards, purple gradients, hero-metric blocks, and "platform" visual language
- Generic AI portfolio patterns: glass cards, gradient text, identical icon-heading-text grids, decorative blur
- Corporate agency sites: stock hero imagery, buzzword carousels, interchangeable case-study layouts
- Anything that reads as category-default (observability-dark-blue, fintech-navy-gold, crypto-neon) rather than this person

## Design Principles

1. **Personality before polish theater.** Prefer one memorable human detail (e.g. what you're listening to) over another stat row or testimonial strip.
2. **Show work, don't pitch it.** Project names and one-liners should sound like how you'd explain them to a friend, not marketing copy.
3. **Indie maker structure.** Lighter hierarchy than a magazine spread when it fights approachability; type and spacing can still be strong, but the page should feel visitable, not ceremonial.
4. **Craft in the margins.** Micro-interactions, type pairing, and grid rhythm earn trust; avoid decoration that does not carry meaning.
5. **Pragmatic access.** Meet WCAG 2.2 AA where it is straightforward (contrast, focus, reduced motion). Do not ship knowingly broken keyboard or motion patterns for aesthetic gain.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Pragmatic baseline: target WCAG 2.2 AA for text contrast, visible focus, and semantic structure on new work. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` (already applied to decorative animation). No formal audit requirement yet; fix obvious barriers when touching a surface.
